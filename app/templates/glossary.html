AirTrack Aviation Glossary

Plain-English explanations for aviation, NOTAM, weather, airspace, and AirTrack module terms.

⚠ Notice: AirTrack is not a certified aviation tool. This glossary is for informational and hobbyist use only. Always use official aviation sources for operational decisions.
Aviation Terms
AGL
Above Ground Level. Altitude measured relative to the terrain directly below the aircraft.
AIP
Aeronautical Information Publication. Official aviation information, procedures, charts, and operational guidance for a country.
ATC
Air Traffic Control. A service that manages aircraft movement on the ground and in controlled airspace.
ATIS
Automatic Terminal Information Service. A recorded broadcast containing airport weather, runway, and operational information.
CASA
Civil Aviation Safety Authority. Australia's aviation safety regulator.
CTAF
Common Traffic Advisory Frequency. A radio frequency used by pilots at non-towered airports.
FIR
Flight Information Region. A defined area of airspace managed by an aviation authority.
FL
Flight Level. A pressure-based altitude reference. Example: FL180 means 18,000 feet.
ICAO
International Civil Aviation Organization. The United Nations aviation body responsible for international standards.
IFR
Instrument Flight Rules. Flight conducted primarily using instruments and ATC procedures.
VFR
Visual Flight Rules. Flight conducted primarily by visual reference outside the aircraft.
NOTAM Terms
NOTAM
Notice to Air Missions. A time-critical aviation notice about operational changes, hazards, restrictions, or outages.
Q-Line
A compact coded NOTAM line containing FIR, subject, condition, traffic, purpose, scope, altitude limits, coordinates, and radius.
SFC
Surface. Used in NOTAM altitude fields to mean ground level.
UNL
Unlimited. Used in NOTAM altitude fields to mean no upper altitude limit.
RWY
Runway. A defined surface used by aircraft for takeoff and landing.
TWY
Taxiway. A path used by aircraft to move between runways, aprons, and parking areas.
CLSD
Closed. Used in NOTAMs to indicate a runway, taxiway, service, or area is unavailable.
Navigation and Weather
DME
Distance Measuring Equipment. A radio navigation system that provides distance information.
GNSS
Global Navigation Satellite System. Satellite navigation systems such as GPS.
GPS Interference
Disruption or degradation of GPS/GNSS signals that may affect navigation systems.
Great Circle Route
A Great Circle Route is the shortest path between two points on the surface of a sphere. Because the Earth is roughly spherical, the shortest route between two airports often appears curved when displayed on a flat map. For example, a flight between Bengaluru and Sydney may appear to take an indirect path when viewed on a map — in reality, the aircraft is following a Great Circle Route that minimises distance and fuel consumption.
ILS
Instrument Landing System. A precision landing aid used during instrument approaches.
Navaid
Navigation Aid. A system assisting aircraft navigation, such as VOR, NDB, ILS, or DME.
SIGMET
Significant Meteorological Information. A weather advisory for hazardous aviation weather.
AirTrack Terms
Aria
AirTrack's interpretation and correlation layer. Aria helps explain patterns but does not replace official aviation sources.
Groundhog Gus
AirTrack's NOTAM and weather scout. Gus receives and normalizes operational notices before passing them to Owl.
Overwatch Owl
AirTrack's operational awareness layer. Owl evaluates urgency, visibility, and escalation.
Ledger Goblin
AirTrack's archival and statistical memory layer. If it happened, he logged it.
